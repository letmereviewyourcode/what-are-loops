## How to record (so it drops in cleanly as a corner PiP)

1. **Framing:** head-and-shoulders, centered, with a little headroom — I'll crop you to a circle/rounded square, so leave space around your head. Keep the same position the whole take.
2. **Look at the lens** (not the screen) when you can, especially on the hook and the final question.
3. **Background & light:** plainish background, light facing you. A warm-ish tone is great — it matches the amber accent.
4. **Length:** record ~**62s** — start talking ~2s of silence in and hold ~2s at the end, so I have trim room.
5. **Sync:** start cue 1's first word **exactly on the teleprompter's "GO"** (it does a 3-2-1 for you). I'll fine-align in the edit, but starting on GO makes it tight. If you can, give a single soft hand-clap right before "GO" — it gives me a clean audio spike to sync to.
6. **Format:** QuickTime → File ▸ New Movie Recording → record → export/save the `.mov` (1080p is plenty). Drop it anywhere in this folder (e.g. `./Me - Zishan/voiceover.mov`) and tell me the path.

## Using the teleprompter

Open **`teleprompter.html`** in a browser (double-click it, or `open teleprompter.html`).

- It plays the rendered video on the left and shows the **current line large** on the right, with the **next line dimmed** below and a **pacing bar** that drains across each line — talk so you finish as the bar empties.
- Press **Space** (or the Start button) → 3-2-1 → **GO**. Read cue 1 on GO.
- **R** resets. The video and the cues are locked to the same clock, so if you watch the video you're automatically on time.
- Tip: do one read-through watching only, then a second take recording. Two or three takes and you'll have it.
